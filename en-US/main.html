Why financial literacy is important

Financial literacy is essential to keep yourself and your family safe in unforeseen circumstances and improve your quality of life. This is important for each of us, in particular, as well as for the state and financial institutions. When people don't make smart financial decisions, consumer and mortgage debt increases, fraudsters rise, crime rates rise, economic stability falls, and people invest in unreliable banks or use questionable instruments to raise their income. The state has to deal with the results: to pay compensation and calm social unrest in society. For example, according to the National Bank, by November 1, 2017, almost 30 thousand applications for refinancing loans (changes in payment terms) were submitted to banks. To support borrowers, a state program was developed: 130 billion tenge was allocated for this from the republican budget. It is worth remembering that the state's money is taxpayers' money, that is, the state eliminates the consequences of low financial literacy at our expense. A logical decision suggests itself: to spend money on improving the financial literacy of the population so as not to deal with the consequences. This approach will pay off in the future.

What is the situation with financial literacy in Kazakhstan

It is difficult to assess the level of financial literacy in Kazakhstan, because there is no high-quality fresh research inside the country. At the global level, such research is carried out by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD). According to her latest research in 2016, which involved 51.5 thousand adults (18 to 79 years old) from 30 countries of the world, only 51-54% of respondents achieved the minimum indicator of financial literacy. Standard & Poors, a financial market analyst, researched financial literacy in 2014. The study involved a sample of 150 thousand people from 148 countries. If you look at the results, it seems that we have everything in order with financial literacy. financial literacy in Russia

Financial literacy in Russia is dealt with by the Ministry of Finance of the Russian Federation, the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, Rospotrebnadzor, the Ministry of Education and Science of Russia, other state (municipal) bodies, financial market institutions and other interested parties.

Sberbank expanded its participation in the Central Bank's project "Online Lessons in Financial Literacy".

In 2016, the OECD published the results of an international comparative study of financial literacy in 30 countries. In Russia, this study was initiated by the Ministry of Finance within the framework of the Project “Assistance in increasing the level of financial literacy of the population and the development of financial education in the Russian Federation”. According to the results of the study, the average score of financial literacy for all countries is 13.2 points out of the maximum 21. In the OECD countries, the average score is 13.7 points. Russia (operator in Russia - NAFI Analytical Center) received 12.2 points (25th place).

In February 2019, it became known that Russia took ninth place among the G20 countries in the rating of the financial literacy of the population. The average indicator of financial literacy among Russians was 12.12 points out of 21. The average score for the G20 countries is 12.7 points.

Financial literacy of children in Russia

According to the research results, almost two-thirds (63%) of children with a bank card know about such a measure of protection against fraudsters as keeping the card separate from cash, 61% know about SMS-informing and the ability to track transactions on a bank card with it.

At the same time (according to a survey by VTsIOM) among 15-17-year-old schoolchildren, every fifth (20%) considers their awareness in the financial and economic sphere to be insufficient and would like to get more extensive knowledge.

Some schools in Russia are introducing a course on the basics of financial literacy.
